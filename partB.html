Part B Major Project

Project Proposal

Iteration of the project along the way is a key part of ensuring a thorough solution to a given problem.

This stage additionally involved several sections of prototyping. Prior to this deadline we undertook a paper prototyping session to test several aspects of our proposed solution.

Qualitative Interviews

I was able to undertake qualitative interviewing with users post-use of our paper prototype in order to assess aspects of the program they found confusing or hard to understand.

Several major navigational issues were identified, which were prioritised. From qualitative interviewing I identified that the burger symbol () was not universally recognised. Additionally, users who were aware of this convention did not what the menu would contain until it was clicked upon.

Numerous user flow issues were uncovered in relation to ensuring that users can perform actions that they consider necessary. I identified that users were confused as how to create, form and the follow a journey. These were addressed in later prototypes.

Coding Tasks

Beginning this semester with very little prior coding experience (started UQ semester 2, 2016), I was very eager to learn a great deal. I threw myself at all challenges and was out of my depth on many occasions.

Coding was largely started after the paper prototyping, which provided valuable insight into improvements which were implemented within the coding.

Menu Bar

In addressing the navigational issues identified within our paper prototyping. We introduced a menu bar which allowed users to easily navigate the website.

One main design point we attempted to make was keeping the screen clutter free, and providing as much space as possible for the map. This led to us to making the menu expandable on hover as shown on the right hand side.

Modals

Another key part from our paper prototyping was that users were unsure of how to navigate the website. We had two options here. Experts often state that a hard to navigate website is the result of poor design choices.

However we felt that due to time constraints we should attempt to implement modals to assist users in navigating their way around the site. These modals were initially quite basic, however in the final version of the website they became more advanced and integral to the first time users experience.
